## What is UFO Editor?

Dive into UFO Editor, the ultimate creative sandbox for Geometry Dash enthusiasts! Developed by the talented Kokim, this fan-made game puts you in the designer's chair, focusing exclusively on the beloved UFO game mode. It's a free-to-play, browser-based rhythm-platformer where the main objective isn't just to survive, but to build. You'll craft devious, gravity-defying flight paths filled with obstacles and game-altering portals. If you've ever played Geometry Dash and wished you could create an entire level dedicated to the UFO's unique, floaty physics, this is the game for you. It strips away the complexity of the full editor, offering a streamlined, accessible toolset perfect for both aspiring creators and seasoned players who want to quickly prototype and share their wildest UFO challenges. Your imagination is the only limit as you design, test, and perfect the ultimate aerial obstacle course.

## How to Play UFO Editor?

Mastering UFO Editor is all about creativity and experimentation. The game is split into two main phases: Designing and Playing.

**Controls:**

- **In-Game (Playing):** Control your UFO with a simple click. Use your **mouse button, spacebar, or tap the screen** to give the UFO a short upward boost. Repeated, timed clicks are key to navigating tight spaces.
- **Editor Mode:** The editor is fully mouse-driven. Select objects like blocks, spikes, and saws from the toolbar. Click on the grid to place them. You can easily select, move, or delete objects to refine your design.

**Gameplay Mechanics:**
Your goal is to build a playable level from start to finish. The magic lies in the special portals. Place **speed portals** to drastically alter the pace, forcing quick reactions. Use **gravity portals** to flip the entire level upside down, and employ **size portals** to shrink the UFO, changing its physics and allowing it to pass through narrow gaps. Once you've placed your objects, hit the **Play button** to instantly test your creation.

**Beginner's Tips:**
Start small. Build a short section with basic jumps to understand the UFO's arc. Then, introduce a single portal, like a speed change, to see how it feels. Don't be afraid to build "impossible" sections and then tweak them until they're challenging but fair. Frequent playtesting is the best way to turn a chaotic idea into a masterpiece.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is UFO Editor an official Geometry Dash game?

No, UFO Editor is a fan-made game created by Kokim. It's a tribute to the original Geometry Dash by RobTop, designed to give players a dedicated space to experiment with the UFO game mode. It is not affiliated with the official developers.

### How do I save and share the levels I create?

After designing your level, the game provides an option to generate a unique 'level code.' You can copy this code and share it with friends or online communities. They can then paste the code into their game to load and play your custom-made challenge.

### What platforms can I play UFO Editor on?

UFO Editor is a browser-based game, best played on a PC or Mac with a mouse for precise editing. While it may function on some mobile browsers, the experience is optimized for desktop to ensure you have full control over object placement.

### How difficult is this game?

The difficulty is entirely up to you! As the creator, you design the challenge. You can make an easy, introductory course or a brutally difficult level that requires frame-perfect precision. The fun comes from finding the right balance between challenging and possible.
