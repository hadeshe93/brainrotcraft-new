## How to Play Geometry Dash Level Editor - 2.0.4?

Mastering the editor is your first step to becoming a legendary creator. The controls are keyboard-focused and designed for efficiency. Press 'E' to open and close the block selection menu, where you'll find everything from basic blocks and spikes to game-changing portals. Use your mouse to click and select the object you want to place. Move your view across the expansive level canvas using the Arrow Keys. When you've found the perfect spot, press the Spacebar to place your selected object. Want to test your creation on the fly? Just press 'P' to jump into playtest mode and see if your timing and traps work as intended. A key feature is the grid system, which you can toggle with 'G' for pixel-perfect alignment.

**Beginner Tips:**
*   **Test Constantly:** Use the 'P' key frequently. It's the best way to feel the flow of your level and find sections that are unfair or don't work.
*   **Save and Share:** Once you're proud of your work, press 'S' to generate a unique level code. Copy this code to save your progress or share it with friends. To load any level, press 'L' and paste the code in.
*   **Start Small:** Don't try to build a massive, epic-length level on your first go. Start with a short, 30-second challenge to learn the fundamentals of good obstacle placement and rhythm.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I save and share my levels?

While in the editor, press the 'S' key. This will generate a long text code that represents your entire level. Copy this code to your clipboard. To share your level, simply send this code to others. To load a level (yours or someone else's), press the 'L' key, paste the code into the pop-up window, and confirm.

### Can I play this on my phone or tablet?

This game is designed for desktop browsers with a physical keyboard. While it might load on a mobile device, the controls (like 'E', 'P', 'S', and arrow keys) are essential for building and testing, making it nearly impossible to use on a touchscreen without a connected keyboard.

### Is this an official Geometry Dash game?

No, this is a non-profit, fan-made tribute created on the Scratch platform by a user named CaptainFlufBall. It lovingly recreates the level editor experience but is not affiliated with the official developer, RobTop Games. It’s a passion project made by a fan, for the fans.

### My level is lagging. What can I do?

Because this is a Scratch game, performance can be limited, especially with very large or object-dense levels. Try to keep your object count reasonable. You can also try playing in a different browser or closing other tabs and applications to free up system resources for a smoother editing and testing experience.
