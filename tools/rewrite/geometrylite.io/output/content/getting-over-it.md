## What is Getting Over It?

This isn't your typical platformer; it's a test of will. In Getting Over It with Bennett Foddy, you play as Diogenes, a man confined to a cauldron who must climb a surreal mountain of discarded objects using only a Yosemite hammer. Your singular goal is to reach the summit. Developed by Bennett Foddy, this physics-based climbing game is a legend in the 'rage game' genre, designed for players who crave a punishing yet deeply rewarding challenge. The game intentionally creates frustration, but every inch of progress feels like a monumental victory. As you climb, Foddy himself provides philosophical commentary on failure and persistence, making your journey a surprisingly introspective one. ## How to Play Getting Over It?

Mastery in Getting Over It comes from a single input: your mouse. Your entire interaction with the world is through the precise movement of the hammer. 

**Basic Controls**:
Move your mouse to swing the hammer in a 360-degree arc around your character. The hammer's head will physically interact with every object it touches, allowing you to push, pull, and hook your way up.

**Core Mechanics**:
The hammer is your everything. You'll need to learn how to:
- **Push**: Plant the hammer against a surface and move the mouse to propel yourself upwards or sideways.
- **Pull**: Hook the hammer's claw onto an edge or object and drag yourself towards it.
- **Swing**: Build momentum by swinging the hammer to launch yourself across gaps.

**Progression and Beginner Tips**:
There are no levels or save points. The game is one continuous, brutal climb. For beginners, remember that **patience is paramount**. Rushed, frantic movements will almost always lead to a fall. Make small, deliberate adjustments and learn to control your momentum. Use the cauldron you sit in to brace against walls, giving you a stable pivot point. Don't be afraid to fail; every fall teaches you something about the physics and the terrain.

## What Makes Getting Over It Special?

Getting Over It stands apart through its sheer, unapologetic difficulty and unique design philosophy. Its most defining feature is the complete lack of checkpoints. This high-stakes design transforms every move into a nail-biting risk, making small successes feel incredibly rewarding and failures devastatingly punishing. Unlike other hard games that test reflexes, this game tests your patience and mental fortitude. Another unique element is the voice-over from creator Bennett Foddy. As you struggle, he muses on frustration, perseverance, and creative inspiration, turning a simple climbing game into a philosophical experience. This combination of brutal gameplay and thoughtful narration created a cultural phenomenon, making it a legendary 'rage game' beloved by streamers and players who seek a challenge that respects their intelligence and tests their resolve.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why is Getting Over It so hard?

The difficulty is intentional. It combines tricky physics, a sensitive single-input control scheme, and the complete absence of checkpoints. One mistake can erase hours of progress, making the journey a true test of patience, precision, and mental fortitude against frustration.

### How long does it take to beat Getting Over It?

This varies dramatically. Some players may conquer the mountain in 5-10 hours, while for others it can take over 50 hours of practice. The median time to finish is famously long, but dedicated speedrunners can now beat it in under two minutes!

### Is there a multiplayer mode in Getting Over It?

No, Getting Over It is a strictly single-player experience. The journey is designed to be a personal one, focusing on your individual struggle and perseverance against the mountain and your own limits.

### What is the best tip for a beginner?

Be patient and make small, deliberate movements. Rushing or making wild swings with the hammer is the fastest way to fall. Take your time to understand how the hammer interacts with different surfaces and learn to control your momentum before attempting big moves.

### What platforms can I play Getting Over It on?

The official game by Bennett Foddy is available on PC (via Steam), macOS, iOS, and Android. Many web-based games have been inspired by its mechanics, allowing you to experience a similar challenge directly in your browser.
