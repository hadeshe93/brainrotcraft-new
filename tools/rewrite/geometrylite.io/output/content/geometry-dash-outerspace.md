## What is Geometry Dash OuterSpace?

Welcome to Geometry Dash OuterSpace, the explosive first level of the fan-favorite Fire Gauntlet. Created by the talented Nicki1202, this Hard-rated, 5-star level launches you into a rhythm-based cosmic challenge. Your mission is to navigate your icon through a sleek, space-themed obstacle course synced perfectly to the pulsating electronic track 'OuterSpace' by Echolox. As a key level within Geometry Dash, the main goal is survival and completion, but the true challenge lies in collecting the three hidden user coins. While its Hard rating might seem daunting, OuterSpace is masterfully designed to be a thrilling entry point for intermediate players looking to test their skills, while still providing a satisfying challenge for veterans. It's the perfect training ground for the fiery trials that await in the rest of the gauntlet.

## How to Play Geometry Dash OuterSpace?

The controls in OuterSpace are elegantly simple, adhering to the classic one-touch gameplay of Geometry Dash. A single click or tap is all you need to act. On a PC, use the spacebar, up arrow, or mouse click. On mobile or web versions, simply tap the screen. Your icon moves automatically, so your job is to time your taps to jump over spikes, navigate tight corridors, and interact with various portals. OuterSpace will test your skills across multiple game modes. You'll start as a cube, requiring precise ground jumps. Soon, you'll transform into a ship, where tapping makes you fly upwards, demanding controlled bursts to maintain altitude. Later, you'll become a ball, where a tap instantly flips your gravity. Pay close attention to jump pads and gravity portals, as they are crucial for navigating the level's complex paths. Progression is linear: survive from 0% to 100%. For beginners, focus on learning the rhythm, as the obstacles are synced to the music. Don't be discouraged by the ship sequence at 14%; make small, controlled taps instead of holding. For the coin at 57%, remember to drop down past the green orb to find the hidden path. When you face the giant monster, focus on the path of its lasers, not the creature itself. Practice Mode is your best friend—use it to place checkpoints and master the trickiest sections.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Geometry Dash OuterSpace good for beginners?

OuterSpace is rated 'Hard' with 5 stars. While not for absolute beginners, it's a fantastic starting point for players who have cleared the official Hard levels. Its clear visual cues and excellent music sync make it easier to learn than many other Hard levels. Use Practice Mode to get comfortable with the trickier ship and ball sections before attempting a normal run.

### Can I play Geometry Dash OuterSpace for free?

OuterSpace is a user-created level within the full, paid version of Geometry Dash. However, due to its popularity, it's often available to play for free on various web-based platforms that host standalone versions of famous Geometry Dash levels. These web versions allow you to experience the level directly in your browser without needing to own the main game, making it highly accessible.

### How do I get the secret coins in OuterSpace?

The three coins require taking secret paths. The first (14%) is in a tight upper passage during the first ship section. The second (57%) is found by dropping below a green orb in the ball section and hitting a blue gravity orb. The third (74%) is located under a laser beam fired by the large monster during the final ship sequence. Each requires precise maneuvering to collect and survive.

### Who made OuterSpace and what is the song?

This iconic level was created by a talented player in the Geometry Dash community known as Nicki1202. The unforgettable, high-energy song that drives the level's rhythm is 'OuterSpace' by the artist Echolox. The perfect sync between the gameplay and the music is one of the main reasons for the level's enduring popularity.
