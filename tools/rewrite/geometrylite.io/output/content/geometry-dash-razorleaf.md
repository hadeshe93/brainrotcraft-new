## What is Geometry Dash Razorleaf?

Geometry Dash Razorleaf is the popular name for the “Poison Gauntlet,” a curated level pack within the official Geometry Dash game. This rhythm-platformer challenge bundles three iconic, fan-made levels into a single, themed experience. Your objective is to navigate your icon through a series of rhythm-based obstacles across all three stages in sequential order. The gauntlet is a journey through lush, neon-infused environments, starting with the tranquil 'Leafwood Forest' and culminating in the intense 'Ocean Paradise Planet.' Created for players who have mastered the basics and are seeking a guided challenge, Razorleaf offers a perfect step up in difficulty. It’s not a standalone game but an official feature available on all platforms where Geometry Dash is sold (PC, macOS, iOS, and Android), designed to test your timing, reflexes, and persistence while rewarding you with exclusive in-game items.

## How to Play Geometry Dash Razorleaf?

Playing the Razorleaf Gauntlet uses standard Geometry Dash controls. On a PC, you'll use a mouse click, spacebar, or the up arrow to jump and activate abilities. On mobile, simply tap the screen. Each tap triggers an action specific to your current form—a jump for the cube, a flap for the ship, a gravity switch for the ball, and so on.

The gauntlet's progression is linear. You must first beat 'Leafwood Forest' (3 stars) to unlock 'Aurora Theory' (5 stars). Completing that level then grants access to the final challenge, 'Ocean Paradise Planet' (7 stars). The core mechanic is timing your actions to the beat of the high-energy music, as the levels are designed to sync perfectly with the soundtrack.

**Beginner Tips:**

1.  **Use Practice Mode:** Before attempting a normal run, use practice mode to place checkpoints and learn the layout of difficult sections without starting over.
2.  **Listen to the Music:** The beat isn't just for atmosphere; it’s a guide. Many jumps and transitions are timed to musical cues.
3.  **Master Each Form:** Each level switches between different forms (cube, ship, wave). Understand how each one controls, especially the tight flying in 'Aurora Theory' and the fast wave sections in 'Ocean Paradise Planet.'
4.  **Stay Patient:** The difficulty ramps up significantly at the final level. Don't get discouraged by crashes—memorization and muscle memory are key to victory.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Geometry Dash Razorleaf a separate game?

No, 'Razorleaf' is the popular community name for the 'Poison Gauntlet,' an official feature within the main Geometry Dash game. You don't need a separate download; you can access it from the Gauntlets menu inside the game.

### What is the difficulty of the Razorleaf Gauntlet?

The gauntlet has a progressive difficulty. It starts with 'Leafwood Forest' (Normal, 3 stars), moves to 'Aurora Theory' (Hard, 5 stars), and ends with the challenging 'Ocean Paradise Planet' (Harder, 7 stars), making it perfect for intermediate players.

### What platforms can I play the Razorleaf Gauntlet on?

Since it's an official part of Geometry Dash, you can play the Poison Gauntlet (aka Razorleaf) on all platforms the game is available on. This includes PC (Steam), macOS, iOS (App Store), and Android (Google Play).

### How do I unlock the gauntlets in Geometry Dash?

To unlock the Gauntlets menu, you first need to collect 10 User Coins. These are special silver coins hidden within many official and online levels. Once you have 10, the Poison Gauntlet will be available to play.

### Any tips for beating the final level, 'Ocean Paradise Planet'?

This 7-star level is a big jump in difficulty. Use Practice Mode extensively to memorize the fast wave and ship sections. The key is to learn the path and develop muscle memory. Pay close attention to the rhythm, as it will help you time your clicks through the tightest corridors.
